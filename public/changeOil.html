Change Oil

Steps

  • Step 1: Park vehicle on level surface, engage parking brake and turn off engine.
  • Step 2: Lift vehicle with a jack and support vehicle with a jack stand.

    Wheel chocks are also recommended to help prevent wheels on the ground from rolling.

  • Step 3: Open vehicle hood and locate engine oil dipstick and remove it.
  • Step 4: Make sure vehicle is safely secure.

    Put on safety glasses and crawl under the vehicle to locate engine's oil pan and oil drain plug. (See owner's manual for reference.)

  • Step 5: Position an oil catch pan under the drain plug and loosen the drain plug using a wrench or socket.

    CAUTION: Oil may be hot!

  • Step 6: Reomve the drain plug and oil will flow rapidly from the hole.

    Once oil is drained out, reinstall oil drain plug and tighten with wrench or socket.

  • Step 7: Locate oil filter. (See vehicle's owner manual for additional information.)

    Loosen oil filter wiith oil filter wrench and remove oil filter.

  • Step 8: Install new oil filter by hand.
  • Step 9: Locate oil fill cap under the vehicle hood and remove it.

    Using a funnel pour in the corrcet amount of oil. (See owner's manual for correct amout of oil.) Reinstall oil fill cap.

  • Step 10: Start the engine and run at idle for minimum of 30 seconds.

    Inspect under the vehicle for oil leaks. If leaks are visible, shut off engine and have leaks repair.

  • Step 11: Lower vehicle. Install and remove oil dipstick to check for proper oil level.

    Add more oil if necessary and close the vehicle hood.
